This module is about action. You have learned what the law says in Level 1. Now you learn exactly what to do when a victim comes to you or when you hear of an atrocity incident in your area. Every hour matters вАФ documentation done in the first 24 hours is worth more than any legal argument later. Speed, accuracy, and compassion are your three tools.

Topic 1 вАФ First Response: Listen, Believe, Document а§µа§ња§Ја§ѓ 1 вАФ а§™а§єа§≤а•А а§™а•На§∞а§§а§ња§Ха•На§∞а§ња§ѓа§Њ: а§Єа•Ба§®а•За§В, а§µа§ња§ґа•На§µа§Ња§Є а§Ха§∞а•За§В, а§¶а§∞а•На§Ь а§Ха§∞а•За§В

When a victim or their family approaches you, your first job is NOT to immediately go to the police. It is to ensure the victim feels safe, is believed, and that everything is accurately recorded before anything else happens. Evidence recorded in the first hours after an incident is the most powerful.

вЬУ DO

  • Listen without interrupting
  • Believe what the victim tells you
  • Write down everything immediately in your diary: date, time, place, what happened, what was said (exact words), names of all witnesses
  • Ask if there are any injuries вАФ arrange for medical treatment first if needed
  • Photograph injuries (with the victim's consent)
  • Collect physical evidence: damaged property, torn clothing, etc.
  • Note the caste-based language used by the accused
  • Ask if there are any witnesses who saw what happened
  • Stay calm and reassure the victim

вЬЧ DO NOT

  • Question whether the victim "provoked" the incident
  • Suggest mediation or compromise with the accused family
  • Share details of the incident with people not involved
  • Promise outcomes you cannot guarantee
  • Handle or move physical evidence without photographing it first
  • Delay going to the police station (every hour of delay helps the accused)
  • Let the accused family persuade the victim to stay silent
  • Take money or gifts in exchange for your assistance
вЪ† Document Caste-Based Language Courts often require proof that the offence was committed because of the victim's caste. Accurately recording the exact words spoken by the accused вАФ especially caste slurs or references to the victim's caste вАФ is critical evidence that cannot be recreated later.

Section 15A (inserted in 2015) is the victim rights charter of the POA Act. As a PLV, you are responsible for making the victim aware of these rights and ensuring they are exercised. Many victims are not told about these rights by the police or prosecution вАФ this is where you make the difference.

The victim must be kept informed about: arrest of the accused, filing of chargesheet, bail applications, trial dates, and judgment. The police and prosecution must update the victim proactively. If they don't, ask the DLSA lawyer to request updates formally.

Field Intervention вАФ Level 2 Quiz

10 questions ¬Ј Pass at 70% ¬Ј Explanations shown after submission
Q1When a victim reports an atrocity to you, what is the FIRST thing you should do?
The first step is to listen, believe, and document вАФ before anything else. Accurate documentation of the incident (including exact caste-based language used) in the first hours is the most powerful evidence. Medical care is parallel if there are injuries.
Q2The SHO tells you: "We need to do a preliminary inquiry before we can register an FIR in this atrocity case." What is the correct response?
Section 18A (2018) explicitly prohibits preliminary inquiry before FIR registration in atrocity cases. Citing this provision and Section 4 (public servant neglect) is the correct first legal response. If the SHO still refuses, then you escalate to SP, Magistrate, and DM in that order.
Q3Under Rule 12, when must the FIRST instalment of compensation reach the victim?
Rule 12 mandates that the first instalment (minimum 25% of total compensation) must be paid within 3-7 days of the incident. This is not dependent on arrest, chargesheet, or conviction вАФ it is automatic once an FIR is registered under the POA Act.
Q4If the police refuse to register an FIR, what is the CORRECT next step after submitting a written complaint to the SHO?
The escalation ladder is: Written complaint to SHO вЖТ SP and Dy.SP вЖТ Magistrate under S.156(3) CrPC вЖТ DM вЖТ National/State SC-ST Commission вЖТ High Court under Article 226. Do not skip steps вАФ each step creates a record and the Magistrate/DM route is often faster than going to the High Court.
Q5Why is it important to take a victim to a government hospital (rather than a private clinic) for their injury examination?
Government medical records are official documents that are harder to challenge in court. Private clinic records can be questioned on credibility. Additionally, the State has a duty to provide free medical treatment to atrocity victims under the POA Act вАФ the government hospital is the correct venue for this.
Q6Section 15A gives the victim the right to be heard when the accused applies for bail. Who must you contact immediately when you learn the accused has applied for bail?
The victim has a statutory right under Section 15A to be heard at bail proceedings. The DLSA lawyer must be informed immediately so they can appear in the Special Court and oppose bail or make submissions on the victim's behalf. Missing a bail hearing can result in the accused being released without the court hearing the victim's perspective.
Q7What is the DVMC and what is its relevance to a PLV's work?
The DVMC (District Vigilance and Monitoring Committee) under Rule 17(3) of the SC/ST (POA) Rules is chaired by the DM/Collector and meets quarterly. It reviews Rule 12 disbursement, rehabilitation, investigation status, Special Court case progress, and victim rights compliance. A PLV can submit written notes on pending cases to be raised at DVMC meetings.
Q8The victim's eyewitness is being threatened by the accused's family and is afraid to testify. What should you do?
Threatening witnesses in atrocity cases is a criminal offence. Report it immediately to the SP and DM. Under the Supreme Court's Witness Protection Scheme 2018, threatened witnesses can receive police protection. The DLSA lawyer should also file an application before the Special Court for witness protection orders.
Q9A PLV's written diary documentation is useful because:
PLV diary notes are valuable as contemporaneous records вАФ they show that the account was documented close in time to the incident, which supports the victim's credibility. A PLV can also testify as a witness to what the victim told them immediately after the incident. These records also support RTI applications and DVMC complaints.
Q10Which of the following is NOT part of a PLV's role in atrocity cases?
A PLV cannot appear in court and cannot argue a case вАФ that is the role of an empanelled DLSA advocate. The PLV's role is field-based: documentation, FIR registration support, compensation application, DLSA liaison, and follow-up. Conflating the PLV role with legal representation can lead to unauthorised practice of law.
