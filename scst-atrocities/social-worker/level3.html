Free Legal Aid — NALSA & DLSA

Under Sec 12(c) of the Legal Services Authorities Act 1987, SC/ST persons are entitled to free legal aid regardless of income. The National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) and District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) are the implementing bodies.

What Free Legal Aid Covers

DLSA — First Point of Contact

District-level body provides: free lawyer in Special Court; assistance drafting FIR/complaint; application for interim relief; accompanying to police station if needed. Contact: District Collector's office or court premises.

NALSA Helpline — 15100

24x7 national helpline for free legal aid referral. Call 15100 to be connected to nearest DLSA. Also handles complaints of legal aid denial.

Para Legal Volunteers (PLVs)

Trained community-level legal aid workers deployed by DLSA — bridge between victim and formal legal system. PLVs can assist with complaint drafting, documentation, and liaison with DLSA.

Field action: As soon as FIR is registered, inform DLSA and request assignment of a Panel Advocate for the Special Court proceedings. Document this request in writing.

DVMC — District Vigilance & Monitoring Committee

The District Vigilance and Monitoring Committee (DVMC) is established under Rule 17(3) of the SC/ST (PoA) Rules. It is a powerful oversight body for monitoring atrocity prevention and relief delivery.

DVMC's Monitoring Duties

DVMC meets quarterly and reviews: pending cases, conviction rates, relief disbursement, ground-level implementation of Act provisions, and sensitisation programmes.

Field worker role: You can approach DVMC with systemic complaints — multiple FIR refusals, pattern of police inaction, delayed relief payments. DVMC can direct SP/DM to act. File written representations before DVMC meetings.

Rehabilitation Schemes for Survivors

Dr. Ambedkar Aawas Navinikaran Yojana

Housing repair/renovation assistance for SC families. Financial support for families displaced by atrocities — apply through District Social Welfare Officer.

MGNREGS Priority Employment

SC/ST atrocity survivors get priority in MGNREGS job cards. District Programme Coordinator must ensure 100 days employment. Useful for economically displaced survivors.

NSKFDC — National SC Finance Corporation

Concessional loans for SC persons displaced by atrocities. Business loans at 6% interest; occupational rehabilitation. Apply through State Channelizing Agency.

State Rehabilitation Schemes

Most states have specific SC/ST rehabilitation packages — Bihar has the SC/ST Kalyan Yojana. Includes: free education for children of victims, ration cards, BPL priority listing, and housing assistance.

Self-Help Groups (SHGs)

Dalit mahila SHGs supported by National Rural Livelihood Mission. Survivors can be linked to SHGs for economic recovery, savings habits, and peer support networks.

📝 Level 3 Assessment — 20 Questions (Pass: 70%)

Q1. Under Rule 12, the total relief for death of an SC/ST victim is:
Total relief for death is ₹8,25,000 — ₹2,00,000 as interim relief and ₹6,25,000 on conviction. This is the highest relief amount under Rule 12.
Q2. Free legal aid for SC/ST persons is guaranteed under:
Sec 12(c) LSA Act 1987 provides that SC/ST persons are entitled to free legal services regardless of income — an absolute entitlement, not means-tested.
Q3. NALSA's national legal aid helpline number is:
NALSA helpline is 15100 — available 24x7 for free legal aid referrals and DLSA connections across India.
Q4. The DVMC (District Vigilance and Monitoring Committee) is established under:
Rule 17(3) of the SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules 1995 establishes DVMC at district level under the chairmanship of the District Magistrate/Collector.
Q5. For a rape case, the interim relief paid on FIR registration is:
For rape, interim relief is 25% of total ₹5,00,000 = ₹1,25,000, payable within 3 months of the offence upon FIR registration.
Q6. Who chairs the DVMC at district level?
The District Magistrate/Collector is the Chairperson of DVMC. The SP is a member, and the District SC/ST Welfare Officer is the Convenor.
Q7. Rule 12 relief for insulting/humiliating an SC/ST person in public is:
Rule 12 provides ₹50,000 for public insult/humiliation offences — ₹25,000 as interim on FIR and ₹25,000 on conviction.
Q8. The NSKFDC provides what type of assistance to displaced SC survivors?
NSKFDC (National Scheduled Castes Finance & Development Corporation) provides concessional loans at 6% for business and occupational rehabilitation of displaced SC persons.
Q9. DVMC meetings should be held how often?
DVMC must meet quarterly — once every three months — to review atrocity cases, conviction rates, relief disbursement, and prevention measures.
Q10. Relief application (Form I) for Rule 12 compensation is submitted to:
Form I (Application for Relief under Rule 12) is filed with the District Magistrate/Collector along with FIR copy, caste certificate, Aadhar, and medical documentation.
Q11. Para Legal Volunteers (PLVs) are deployed by which body?
PLVs are deployed by DLSA under the Legal Services Authorities framework — they are trained community volunteers who bridge victims with formal legal aid services.
Q12. The total Rule 12 relief for grievous permanent disability is:
Grievous permanent disability is equated with death in Rule 12 — total relief is ₹8,25,000 — ₹2,00,000 interim on FIR and balance on conviction.
Q13. SC/ST children whose parents are atrocity victims are entitled to priority in:
State rehabilitation packages under schemes like Bihar SC/ST Kalyan Yojana include free education for children of victims, ration card priority, BPL listing, and housing assistance.
Q14. A field worker can approach DVMC to raise complaints about:
DVMC is appropriate for systemic complaints — patterns of police inaction, multiple FIR refusals, delayed relief disbursement — not individual civil disputes.
Q15. MGNREGS priority employment for atrocity survivors must ensure:
SC/ST atrocity survivors get priority access to the standard 100 days MGNREGS employment — the District Programme Coordinator is responsible for ensuring this priority access.
Q16. For a social boycott offence, the total Rule 12 relief is:
Social boycott attracts ₹1,00,000 total relief — ₹50,000 as interim on FIR and ₹50,000 on conviction.
Q17. Which scheme provides housing assistance to SC families damaged/displaced by atrocities?
Dr. Ambedkar Aawas Navinikaran Yojana provides housing repair/renovation assistance specifically for SC families — apply through District Social Welfare Officer.
Q18. The balance Rule 12 compensation (after interim relief) is paid:
The balance (remaining percentage after interim relief) is typically paid after conviction — but courts have ordered full relief even in cases of acquittal where victim's identity was established.
Q19. Which documents are essential for filing Form I (Rule 12 relief application)?
Form I requires: FIR copy (with station diary number), SC/ST caste certificate (government issued), Aadhar for identity, and MLC/medical certificate documenting the injury/offence.
Q20. Dalit women survivors can be linked to ___ for economic recovery and peer support:
National Rural Livelihood Mission (NRLM/Jeevika) supports Dalit mahila SHGs — survivors can be linked for savings, peer support, loans, and economic rehabilitation.
