Level 2: First Response & FIR Support

FIR anatomy, mandatory arrest, police escalation, medical examination, interim relief under Rule 12, and victim protection under Sec 15A

The FIR: 6 Critical Elements

An FIR (First Information Report) is mandatory when a cognizable atrocity offence is reported. All SC/ST atrocity offences are cognizable — police cannot refuse to register the FIR.

1

Date, Time and Place of Offence

Exact location — village/ward/district; approximate time and date; any witnesses present

2

Identity of Victim and Caste Mention

Name, village, caste/SC/ST community with sub-caste; must explicitly state complainant's SC/ST identity

3

Identity of Accused and Non-SC/ST Status

Name(s) of accused, their caste (non-SC/ST), village; establish the inter-community element clearly

4

Specific Sections of POA Act

Name the exact section violated — e.g., "Sec 3(1)(x) — intentional insult in public view" — do not rely on police to identify correct sections

5

Nature of Offence in Detail

What happened, how, with what weapon/means; any words used; any humiliation/injury; witnesses' names

6

Prayer/Relief Sought

Request for arrest under Sec 18A; request for interim relief under Rule 12; request for DLSA legal aid

Field tip: Always get a copy of the FIR with station diary number. If police refuse, note the refusal in writing, note the officer's name/badge number, and escalate immediately to DSP/SP.

Mandatory Arrest — Section 18A (2016 Amendment)

The 2016 Amendment inserted Sec 18A which mandates that for atrocity offences, the police officer SHALL arrest the accused and NO anticipatory bail shall be granted.

No Anticipatory Bail: Section 18 POA Act bars Courts from granting anticipatory bail for atrocity offences. The Supreme Court in Prathvi Raj Chauhan v. UOI (2020) confirmed this — Sec 18A is constitutionally valid and mandatory arrest applies.

What to Do If Police Delay Arrest

1

Written Complaint to SP/DSP

File written complaint citing Sec 18A — mandatory duty to arrest. Document the FIR number, accused name, and days elapsed.

2

Application to Special Court / Magistrate

File application for direction to police to arrest; Magistrate can pass directions under Sec 156(3) CrPC

3

Complaint to State SC/ST Cell

Most states have a dedicated SC/ST cell at police HQ — written complaint attracts supervisory scrutiny

4

NCSC / NCST Complaint

National Commission for Scheduled Castes (NCSC) and National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST) can issue directions and summon officials

Medical Examination — When and How

For sexual offences and physical injuries, immediate medical examination is critical for preserving evidence. Rule 11 of POA Rules mandates prompt medical care.

IssueProtocol
Female survivorExamination MUST be by a female medical officer
MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate)Document all injuries with dimensions, nature, cause; must mention "alleged POA offence"
Rape/sexual assaultTwo-finger test is PROHIBITED (SC ruling); DNA preservation is mandatory
Mental traumaPsychological assessment can be requested; mental harm is a compensable injury
TimingDo not delay — evidence degrades; police cannot refuse to facilitate medical exam
Field action: If police delay medical exam, take the survivor directly to a government hospital and ask for MLC under POA offence. Hospital cannot refuse emergency medical care.

Interim Relief — Rule 12 (Selected Key Amounts)

Under Rule 12 of SC/ST (PoA) Rules 1995, interim relief must be provided immediately upon FIR registration — do not wait for conviction or charge-sheet.

OffenceInterim ReliefTimeline
Death of victim₹2,00,000 (50% of total ₹8,25,000)Within 2 months of death
Rape₹1,25,000 (25% of total ₹5,00,000)Within 3 months of offence
Gang Rape₹2,06,250 (25% of total ₹8,25,000)Within 3 months
Hurt causing grievous injury₹50,000 (50% of ₹1,00,000)Within 1 month

Dispossession from landRestoration of possession + ₹25,000Immediately
Who pays: State government must pay relief from the SC/ST Development Fund — NOT the accused. Relief is paid regardless of conviction. Your role is to help victim apply for relief to the District Magistrate/Collector.

Victim Rights — Section 15A (2016 Amendment)

Sec 15A, inserted by the 2016 Amendment, is the most comprehensive victim protection provision in Indian criminal law. It guarantees multiple rights to atrocity victims and witnesses.

Key Rights Under Sec 15A

RightDetail
Right to be informedVictim must be informed about progress of investigation, charge-sheet filing, trial dates
Right to be heardVictim has right to be heard at every stage — bail hearing, discharge, plea bargaining
Protection of identityName/address of victim and witnesses NOT to be disclosed in media or public
Safety and securityState must provide reasonable protection to victim, witnesses and their families
Relocation if neededVictim/witnesses can be relocated for safety at state expense
Video-conferencing testimonyVictims/witnesses can give evidence through video-conferencing to avoid intimidation

📝 Level 2 Assessment — 20 Questions (Pass: 70%)

Q1. All atrocity offences under the POA Act are:
All atrocity offences are cognizable — police must register FIR without requiring a Magistrate's order and must investigate immediately.
Q2. Which section mandates arrest without anticipatory bail for atrocity offences?
Sec 18A, inserted by 2016 Amendment, mandates arrest and prohibits anticipatory bail for atrocity offences. Upheld in Prathvi Raj Chauhan (2020).
Q3. When a female survivor reports sexual assault under POA Act, the medical examination must be conducted by:
Female survivor's medical examination must be done by a female medical officer — this is mandatory protocol and refusal to comply is itself a violation.
Q4. Interim relief for death of an SC/ST victim must be paid within:
Rule 12 prescribes that interim relief for death must be paid within 2 months of the death — NOT after conviction. Relief is independent of trial outcome.
Q5. The two-finger test during medical examination of rape survivors is:
The Supreme Court in Lillu v. State of Haryana (2013) and later in Independent Thought v. UOI banned the two-finger test as it violates dignity and is medically meaningless.
Q6. The interim relief for rape under Rule 12 is what percentage of the total compensation?
Rule 12 provides 25% of total compensation as interim relief for rape (₹1,25,000 of total ₹5,00,000), payable within 3 months of the offence.
Q7. Sec 15A of the POA Act (2016 Amendment) primarily deals with:
Sec 15A is the victim rights chapter — it guarantees rights to information, being heard, identity protection, safety, relocation and video-conferencing testimony.
Q8. An FIR must specifically mention which element to attract POA Act provisions?
The FIR must establish the caste identity of both victim (SC/ST) and accused (non-SC/ST) — this inter-community element is the jurisdictional prerequisite for POA Act application.
Q9. Interim relief under Rule 12 is paid by:
State government pays relief from the SC/ST Development Fund — it is independent of trial and conviction. The victim receives relief even if the accused is acquitted.
Q10. If police refuse to register FIR for an atrocity, what is the FIRST escalation step?
Immediate escalation to SP/DSP with a written complaint documenting the refusal — this triggers their supervisory duty. Magistrate complaint under Sec 156(3) CrPC is another simultaneous option.
Q11. Under Sec 15A, victims can give evidence through ___ to avoid face-to-face intimidation:
Sec 15A specifically provides for video-conferencing testimony to protect victims and witnesses from intimidation during court appearances.
Q12. Which court has jurisdiction to try atrocity offences?
The POA Act mandates Exclusive Special Courts for trial — Sessions Courts can also be designated as Special Courts. This ensures faster trials.
Q13. MLC in the context of a medical examination stands for:
MLC — Medico-Legal Certificate — documents all physical injuries with dimensions, nature, probable cause, and is a critical piece of evidence in atrocity cases.
Q14. Relief for dispossession from government-allotted land under Rule 12 includes:
Rule 12 for land dispossession cases requires both restoration of possession and monetary compensation (minimum ₹25,000) — restoration is the primary remedy.
Q15. Under Sec 15A, the state must protect the ___ of the victim and witnesses:
Sec 15A mandates that names and addresses of victims and witnesses shall not be disclosed to the media or in public proceedings — identity protection is a fundamental right under this section.
Q16. How many elements are critical in a well-drafted FIR for an atrocity offence?
A complete atrocity FIR has 6 elements: date/time/place; victim's SC/ST identity; accused's non-SC/ST identity; specific POA sections; detailed nature of offence; and specific prayer/relief sought.
Q17. The Supreme Court ruling in Prathvi Raj Chauhan (2020) held that Sec 18A is:
Prathvi Raj Chauhan v. UOI (2020) SC 5-judge bench upheld Sec 18A — restored mandatory arrest and no anticipatory bail for atrocity offences, reversing the earlier Subhash Kashinath Mahajan dilution.
Q18. Interim relief under Rule 12 is payable:
Relief under Rule 12 is payable on FIR registration — it does not wait for charge-sheet or conviction. The state pays from development funds regardless of trial outcome.
Q19. Victims can be relocated by the state under Sec 15A when:
Sec 15A provides for relocation of victims and witnesses at state expense when their safety is threatened as a result of the atrocity or their participation in the criminal process.
Q20. A field worker's primary duty when accompanying a survivor to the police station is to:
The field worker ensures: FIR registered with correct POA sections; inter-community element mentioned; copy obtained; immediate escalation path identified if refused. Settlement/compromise of atrocity offences is illegal.
