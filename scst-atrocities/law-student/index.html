📋 Case Law Quick Reference — 8 Landmark Cases

CaseYearCourtKey Ratio / Principle
Subhash Kashinath Mahajan v. State of Maharashtra 2018 SC Added preliminary inquiry and anticipatory bail — overridden entirely by 2018 Amendment (§18A). The override is itself a landmark constitutional event — Parliament naming the case in legislation and reversing it.
Lalita Kumari v. Govt. of UP 2014 SC (5-judge CB) FIR registration is mandatory for cognizable offences — no police discretion, no preliminary inquiry. Applies with even greater force to SC/ST Act cases where §18A additionally bars preliminary inquiry.
Rudul Shah v. State of Bihar 1983 SC Constitutional compensation for fundamental rights violations — established doctrine of constitutional tort. Courts can award compensation under Art.32/226 even without statutory basis where fundamental rights are violated by State.
Nilabati Behera v. State of Orissa 1993 SC State is strictly liable for constitutional violations — no private tort defences available. Constitutional compensation is a public law remedy, not private tort. Standard: no need to prove malice or intent of state actors.
Patan Jamal Vali v. State of AP 2021 SC Intersectionality — caste + disability + gender compound vulnerability. Courts must recognise multiple axes of marginalisation in sentencing and relief. Transformative constitutionalism applied to SC/ST Act.
Gurinder Singh Aujla v. State of Punjab 2020 SC §9 DSP investigation is mandatory, not directory. Investigation by sub-DSP rank officer = procedurally invalid, challengeable before Special Court. Always check IO's rank on the FIR at the outset of every case.
Mahender Chawla v. UOI 2019 SC Anticipatory bail bar under §18 confirmed absolute. 2018 Amendment (§18A) constitutionally valid. Very narrow exception only where complaint is manifestly false on face of record — not a general power to grant anticipatory bail.
State of Karnataka v. Appa Balu Ingale 1993 SC Art.17 directly enforceable against private individuals. Denial of SC/ST persons' access to common well (and by extension any public resource) on caste basis = atrocity. SC restored conviction reversed by HC.
