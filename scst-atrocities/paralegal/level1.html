PCR Act 1955 — Complete Scheme

The Protection of Civil Rights Act 1955 is the successor to the Untouchability (Offences) Act 1955. It penalises every form of disability enforced on grounds of untouchability.

Paralegal tip: PCR Act offences are cognizable and non-bailable under Sec 13 — FIR registration is mandatory. However, PCR cases are typically tried by ordinary magistrate courts, unlike POA Act which has Special Courts.

POA Act 1989 — Complete Statutory Scheme

Chapter I — Preliminary (Sec 1-2)

Sec 2(a) "Atrocity" means an offence punishable under Sec 3(1) or 3(2). Sec 2(b) "Code" means the Code of Criminal Procedure 1973. Sec 2(c) "Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes" as defined under Arts. 341 and 342 of the Constitution.

2016 Amendment — Critical New Provisions

Sec 15A (Victim & Witness Rights): Right to be informed, heard, protected; identity protection; state safety measures; relocation; video-conferencing testimony; right to engage their own advocate.

Sec 18A (Mandatory Arrest): No preliminary inquiry before registration; no prior approval from SP for arrest; charge-sheet within 60 days.

Chapter IVA — Rights of Victims & Witnesses (Sec 15A): The most comprehensive victim protection provision in Indian criminal law.

Prathvi Raj Chauhan v. Union of India (2020) 4 SCC 727

5-judge Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court:

  • Upheld the constitutionality of Sec 18A of the POA Act
  • Reversed the earlier Subhash Kashinath Mahajan (2018) ruling that had diluted the Act
  • Held: No preliminary inquiry; no SP approval for arrest; No anticipatory bail — all restored as mandatory
  • Confirmed that Art. 21 rights of accused do not override special protection for SC/ST communities

📝 Level 1 Assessment — 20 Questions (Pass: 70%)

Q1. PCR Act offences under Sec 13 are classified as:
PCR Act Sec 13 classifies all offences as cognizable and non-bailable — mandatory FIR registration and no right to bail as of right.
Q2. Which PCR Act section presumes untouchability motive when disability is enforced against an SC person?
PCR Act Sec 14 creates a presumption — if a disability is enforced against an SC person, the court shall presume the motive was untouchability, shifting the burden of proof to the accused.
Q3. Under the post-2016 renumbering, rape of an SC/ST woman falls under:
Sec 3(2)(v) covers rape and gang rape of SC/ST women by non-SC/ST persons — minimum 6 years imprisonment, in addition to IPC rape provisions.
Q4. Sec 16 of the POA Act allows the imposition of:
Sec 16 empowers the state to impose a collective fine on an entire locality/community if the atrocity was committed with their knowledge, connivance, or abetment without informing police.
Q5. Rule 7 requires investigation to be conducted by an officer not below the rank of:
Rule 7 mandates investigation by an officer not below the rank of DSP — this ensures experienced officers handle atrocity cases, not low-ranking constables who may be biased.
Q6. Dedicating an SC/ST woman as a devadasi is covered under:
Sec 3(1)(p) covers the practice of dedicating SC/ST women as devadasis or similar customary practice — recognizes this as a form of sexual exploitation and social atrocity.
Q7. Charge-sheet in atrocity cases must be filed within how many days of FIR under Rule 7?
Rule 7 mandates filing of charge-sheet within 60 days of FIR registration — a paralegal can use RTI or application to Special Court to ensure this deadline is met.
Q8. The State-level Vigilance and Monitoring Committee under Rule 18 is chaired by:
Rule 18 constitutes a State-level Vigilance and Monitoring Committee chaired by the Chief Minister — the highest political accountability mechanism under the POA Rules.
Q9. Social or economic boycott of an SC/ST person or community falls under:
Sec 3(1)(q) specifically criminalises social and economic boycott — covering situations where the community is denied services, markets, or social interactions collectively.
Q10. The Prathvi Raj Chauhan (2020) SC ruling reversed which earlier dilutive judgment?
Subhash Kashinath Mahajan (2018) had introduced preliminary inquiry requirements and SP approval for arrest — Prathvi Raj Chauhan (2020) reversed both and restored the Act's mandatory provisions.
Q11. Sec 14A POA Act provides for the establishment of:
Sec 14A allows states to establish Exclusive Special Courts — entirely dedicated to atrocity cases — where volume of cases warrants it, ensuring faster disposal.
Q12. Preventing SC/ST persons from participation in panchayat/election proceedings is covered by:
Sec 3(1)(v) covers preventing SC/ST persons from exercising their political rights — standing for election, voting, participating in panchayat proceedings.
Q13. Sec 18A (2016 Amendment) requires that the charge-sheet be filed within:
Sec 18A read with Rule 7 requires the charge-sheet to be filed within 60 days of FIR registration — a binding deadline that can be monitored by paralegals through RTI.
Q14. Rule 5 requires the District Magistrate to identify and notify:
Rule 5 mandates DM to identify atrocity-prone areas and notify them — this triggers enhanced surveillance, biweekly SP visits, and preventive action under Rule 6.
Q15. Sec 21 POA Act imposes a duty on the Government to submit a report on implementation to:
Sec 21 mandates the Government to annually report to Parliament on all measures taken for effective implementation of the POA Act — creating legislative accountability.
Q16. Forcing someone to defecate in the open (manual scavenging-adjacent offence) is covered under:
Sec 3(1)(n) — added by 2016 Amendment — covers compelling SC/ST persons to dispose of or carry human or animal remains and forcing to do filthy/unclean work including open defecation.
Q17. Rule 13 of SC/ST PoA Rules covers:
Rule 13 covers rehabilitation assistance — restoration of agricultural land, housing construction, MGNREGS employment priority, transport of SHG goods — for displaced or damaged SC/ST persons.
Q18. Sec 19 POA Act provides protection to persons who act:
Sec 19 provides immunity from civil suit or legal proceedings to persons acting in good faith in execution of powers or duties under the POA Act — protects officials and advocates from vexatious litigation.
Q19. Damaging or desecrating property of religious significance to SC/ST falls under:
Sec 3(1)(m) covers damaging or desecrating property or objects of religious significance to SC/ST persons — temples, deities, sacred objects, burial grounds.
Q20. A Special Public Prosecutor under Sec 14B is appointed by:
Sec 14B mandates the State Government to appoint a Special Public Prosecutor for each Special Court — ensuring a dedicated prosecutor with expertise handles atrocity cases.
