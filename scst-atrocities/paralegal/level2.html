Level 2: Complaint Drafting & Special Courts

Drafting technically sound FIRs and magistrate complaints, navigating Special Court procedures, Sec 18A mandatory arrest, witness rights and police accountability mechanisms

Drafting an Atrocity Complaint — Model Structure

A well-drafted complaint is the foundation of a successful atrocity prosecution. As a paralegal, you must ensure all essential legal elements are present.

To, The Officer-in-Charge, [Police Station Name], [District] Sub: Complaint under SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989 & Protection of Civil Rights Act 1955 Respected Sir/Madam, I, [VICTIM'S FULL NAME], son/daughter of [Father's Name], resident of [Full address with village/ward, block, district], belonging to [SC/ST community name] Scheduled Caste/Tribe (as notified under Art. 341/342 of the Constitution), do hereby state as follows: FACTS OF THE OFFENCE: On [DATE] at approximately [TIME], at [EXACT LOCATION], the accused [ACCUSED FULL NAME(S)], son/daughter of [Father's Name], resident of [Address], caste: [NON-SC/ST CASTE NAME], committed the following atrocity against me: [DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT — what happened, how, words used, weapons/means, injuries, witnesses present] SECTIONS APPLICABLE: The above conduct attracts the following provisions: 1. Section 3(1)[__] SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act 1989 [State the specific sub-section and its description] 2. [Any additional POA Act sections] 3. IPC Section [__] [if applicable alongside POA offence] PRAYER: I humbly request you to: 1. Register FIR under the above provisions forthwith 2. Arrest the accused under Sec 18A of the POA Act 3. Conduct investigation through a DSP-rank officer (Rule 7) 4. Initiate interim relief proceedings under Rule 12 5. Refer me to DLSA for free legal aid under LSA Act 1987 [WITNESS NAMES AND ADDRESSES] Yours faithfully, [VICTIM'S SIGNATURE / THUMB IMPRESSION] [DATE]
Critical elements a paralegal must verify: (1) Caste of victim explicitly stated as SC/ST; (2) Caste of accused stated as non-SC/ST; (3) Specific POA section cited; (4) Prayer includes Sec 18A arrest and Rule 12 relief; (5) Witness names and addresses included.

Special Courts — Jurisdiction & Procedure

Cognizance Without Committal (Sec 15)

A unique feature — Special Courts under the POA Act can take direct cognizance of atrocity offences without requiring committal proceedings from a Magistrate. This bypasses the normal CrPC procedure and speeds up trial.

Trial Procedure in Special Courts

1

Cognizance

Special Court takes cognizance either on police report (charge-sheet) or on complaint — no committal required (Sec 15)

2

Charges Framed

Court frames charges against accused; victim/PLV should verify correct POA sections are charged — not just IPC sections

3

Evidence Stage

Prosecution (Special PP) examines witnesses; defense cross-examination. PLV role: ensure victim knows their right to be heard (Sec 15A)

4

Victim Hearing at Bail

Sec 15A — victim must be heard before bail is granted to accused. PLV must alert victim of bail hearing dates immediately

5

Judgment & Sentence

Conviction must trigger automatic application for balance Rule 12 compensation. File within 30 days of conviction order.

Paralegal duty at Special Court: File vakalatnama for victim's private advocate (Sec 15A right to engage own lawyer); attend all dates; ensure victim is informed of each hearing; file Form I for Rule 12 relief immediately after conviction.

Sec 18A — Mandatory Arrest: Drafting an Arrest Application

When police delay or refuse arrest despite FIR registration, a paralegal can draft and file an application to the SP citing Sec 18A and the constitutional mandate.

To, The Superintendent of Police, [District], [State] Sub: Application under Section 18A SC/ST (PoA) Act 1989 for immediate arrest of accused Sir, FIR No. [___] dated [DATE], registered at PS [NAME], under Sections [___] SC/ST (PoA) Act 1989, names the following accused: 1. [NAME], son of [FATHER], Village [___], Caste: [NON-SC/ST] As of today [DATE], the accused has NOT been arrested despite [X] days having elapsed since FIR registration. Section 18A of the SC/ST (PoA) Act 1989 (inserted by 2016 Amendment) mandates that: (a) No preliminary inquiry shall be required prior to FIR registration; (b) The accused SHALL be arrested; and (c) No sanction of SP is required. The Supreme Court in Prathvi Raj Chauhan v. UOI (2020) 4 SCC 727 confirmed that Sec 18A is constitutionally valid and mandatory. Non-arrest despite the mandate of Sec 18A constitutes wilful neglect under Sec 4 POA Act, punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year. I therefore request you to: 1. Direct immediate arrest of the accused under Sec 18A 2. Initiate departmental action against SHO [NAME] for inaction 3. Acknowledge receipt of this complaint Complainant: [VICTIM NAME], [ADDRESS] Through PLV: [YOUR NAME], DLSA [DISTRICT] Date: [DATE]

Witness Rights & Protection

Witness intimidation is common in atrocity cases. Sec 15A provides the following protections that a paralegal must be aware of and help enforce:

RightHow to Enforce
Identity protection — no media disclosureFile complaint to SP if media publishes victim/witness name; Sec 15A is enforceable
Video-conferencing testimonyApply to Special Court for order permitting VC testimony if witness faces threat
State securityApplication to SP for police protection to witness's home; escalate to DM if refused
RelocationApplication to DM citing Sec 15A for temporary or permanent relocation of victim/witness
Travel expensesState must pay travel/maintenance expenses for witness attending court — apply to Special PP
Harassment of witness is a fresh offence: Sec 3(2)(vii) makes it an atrocity offence to harass a victim/witness/dependent after filing of complaint — a new FIR can be filed for post-complaint harassment.

📝 Level 2 Assessment — 20 Questions (Pass: 70%)

Q1. Special Courts under POA Act can take cognizance of atrocity offences:
Sec 15 POA Act allows Special Courts to take direct cognizance without requiring committal proceedings from a Magistrate — this speeds up trial significantly.
Q2. A complaint under POA Act can be filed directly with a Magistrate when:
After exhausting police and SP routes, a complaint to the Magistrate under Sec 156(3) CrPC directing FIR and investigation is the next step — the Magistrate has jurisdiction to order police to register FIR.
Q3. What is the critical inter-community element that must appear in every atrocity complaint?
The jurisdictional prerequisite for POA Act is the inter-community element — both victim's SC/ST identity and accused's non-SC/ST identity must be stated clearly in the complaint/FIR.
Q4. Post-complaint harassment of a victim/witness constitutes a fresh atrocity under:
Sec 3(2)(vii) makes it a separate atrocity offence to harass a victim/witness/dependent after filing of complaint — this enables a fresh FIR for post-complaint intimidation.
Q5. A victim's right to be heard at the bail hearing of the accused is guaranteed under:
Sec 15A guarantees the victim's right to be heard at every stage of proceedings including bail hearings — the PLV must ensure the victim is informed of all bail hearing dates.
Q6. Wilful neglect of duty by a police officer who refuses to register FIR attracts prosecution under:
Sec 4 POA Act — wilful neglect of duties — carries up to 1 year's imprisonment. This section is enforceable against any public servant (police, magistrate, revenue official) who deliberately ignores their duties.
Q7. A well-drafted atrocity complaint must include as Prayer:
A complete prayer covers all five points: (1) FIR registration; (2) Arrest under Sec 18A; (3) DSP-level investigation per Rule 7; (4) Interim relief under Rule 12; (5) DLSA referral for free legal aid.
Q8. Under Sec 15A, travel expenses for witnesses attending court must be paid by:
Sec 15A mandates that the state bear travel and maintenance expenses for victims and witnesses attending court — apply to the Special PP or the court registry for disbursement.
Q9. Under Sec 18A, the SP's approval for arrest in atrocity cases is:
Sec 18A explicitly provides that no sanction or approval of SP or other officer is required before arrest in atrocity cases — the station officer must arrest without seeking higher approval.
Q10. After conviction in an atrocity case, the paralegal's immediate duty is to:
On conviction, the balance of Rule 12 compensation becomes immediately claimable — file Form I with the DM within 30 days of the conviction order to initiate payment.
Q11. Identity of victim and witnesses must NOT be disclosed to:
Sec 15A mandates that names and addresses of victims and witnesses shall not be disclosed to the media or in public — violation of this is itself an offence and a grievance to be filed before the SP/DM.
Q12. A vakalatnama filed by the victim's private advocate in Special Court is authorised by:
Sec 15A explicitly grants victims the right to engage their own advocate in addition to the Special PP — the victim's private lawyer can appear and represent their interests alongside the state prosecutor.
Q13. If the Special Court charges only IPC sections and omits POA sections, a paralegal should:
Filing an application for amendment of charges is the immediate remedy — ensuring POA Act sections are charged is critical because the enhanced punishments and victim relief provisions only apply if POA sections are in the charge sheet.
Q14. The Sec 156(3) CrPC application to Magistrate is appropriate when:
After police refusal and SP escalation failure, Sec 156(3) CrPC allows the Magistrate to direct the police to register FIR and investigate — a powerful tool for paralegal use.
Q15. When drafting an FIR/complaint, the caste of the accused must be stated as:
The accused's specific caste name must be stated and it must be established that they are NOT an SC or ST — this inter-community element is the jurisdictional basis for POA Act application.
Q16. For witnesses facing threat, Sec 15A allows testimony through:
Apply to the Special Court for a video-conferencing order under Sec 15A — the court can allow witnesses to testify remotely to avoid face-to-face confrontation with the accused or their supporters.
Q17. NCSC (National Commission for Scheduled Castes) can be approached when:
NCSC (under Art. 338) is appropriate for systemic complaints — pattern of police inaction, state complicity, or high-level officials failing their statutory duties under POA Act.
Q18. The model complaint must include the prayer for Rule 7 investigation by:
Rule 7 mandates investigation by an officer not below the rank of DSP — the prayer must specifically request DSP-level investigation to prevent the case being handed to sub-inspector level officers.
Q19. An atrocity complaint should NOT include a request for:
Atrocity offences are non-compoundable — they cannot be settled or compromised. Including a request for compromise would undermine the complaint and is legally impermissible.
Q20. A paralegal's duty to the victim at Special Court includes ensuring:
The PLV's core duty at Special Court: communicate all dates; file vakalatnama for private advocate; ensure Sec 15A rights (right to be heard, identity protection) are exercised; monitor bail hearings; file Rule 12 applications on conviction.
