Level 3: RTI, Compensation & DLSA Access

Step-by-step Rule 12 claim process, drafting RTI applications to track case progress, navigating NALSA/DLSA services, and engaging DVMC for community-level accountability

Rule 12 Claim — Step-by-Step Process

1

Obtain FIR Copy

Collect certified copy of FIR from police station (paid for or free under RTI). Verify POA sections are correctly cited.

2

Obtain SC/ST Caste Certificate

From Tehsildar/SDM — original or certified copy. This proves the victim's SC/ST identity as a jurisdictional prerequisite.

3

Obtain MLC / Medical Certificate

From government hospital — documents injuries for physical offences; required for rape, hurt, and death cases.

4

Draft Form I Application

Form I (Application for Relief under SC/ST PoA Rules) addressed to District Magistrate/Collector. Attach all documents.

5

Submit to DM/Collector

Submit at DM office — obtain stamped acknowledgment with date. Keep receipt safe — use it to track response.

6

Follow Up — RTI if No Response

If DM does not respond within 30 days, file RTI to district welfare office asking for status of Form I application.

7

Escalate Delays to DVMC

File written representation before DVMC meeting citing delay. DVMC can direct DM to act on pending relief applications.

Timeline tracker for paralegals: Death case — interim relief within 2 months. Rape — within 3 months. Grievous hurt — within 1 month. For arson and dispossession — immediate possession restoration first, then monetary relief.

Model RTI Application — Charge-Sheet Status

To, The Public Information Officer, Office of the Superintendent of Police, [District], [State] Application under Section 6 of the Right to Information Act 2005 Subject: Information regarding investigation and charge-sheet in FIR No. [___] dated [DATE], PS [STATION NAME] I hereby seek the following information: 1. Current status of investigation in the above FIR 2. Name and rank of Investigating Officer currently in charge 3. Date on which charge-sheet (if any) has been filed in Special Court 4. If charge-sheet not filed: reasons for non-filing given Rule 7 SC/ST (PoA) Rules 1995 which mandates filing within 60 days 5. Whether the accused has been arrested; if not, reasons for non-arrest given Section 18A SC/ST (PoA) Act 1989 6. Whether any preliminary inquiry was conducted before FIR registration (which is prohibited under Sec 18A) I request certified copies of: (a) Case diary entries from [DATE of FIR] to present (b) Any written orders from supervisory officers regarding this case Applicant: [NAME], [ADDRESS] Date: [DATE] Fee: ₹10/- (or as applicable) [Attach IPO/court fee stamp]

DVMC Engagement — Paralegal's Role

The District Vigilance and Monitoring Committee (DVMC) is a quarterly accountability body. As a PLV, you can leverage it for systemic complaints and policy advocacy.

How to Engage DVMC

1

Find DVMC Meeting Dates

File RTI with DM office asking for DVMC meeting schedule for the year — they are mandated to meet quarterly.

2

Submit Written Representation

Send written representation to DM office (Chairperson, DVMC) 7 days before meeting with specific complaints: pending FIRs, delayed relief, police inaction.

3

Request to Attend as NGO Member

Rule 17(3) provides for NGO/civil society representatives on DVMC — apply formally to DM to be included as a member from your organisation.

4

Follow Up on Action Taken Report (ATR)

After each DVMC meeting, file RTI for minutes and Action Taken Report — ensures accountability for commitments made.

📝 Level 3 Assessment — 20 Questions (Pass: 70%)

Q1. RTI applications to monitor atrocity investigations are filed with the:
RTI applications go to the designated PIO of the relevant department — SP office for investigation status, DM office for relief applications, Special Court registry for trial dates.
Q2. RTI can be used to enforce the 60-day charge-sheet deadline by seeking information from:
RTI to SP office PIO asking for charge-sheet date and reasons for non-filing (if beyond 60 days) creates a documented record that can be used before the Magistrate or SP for escalation.
Q3. Section 357A CrPC (Section 395 BNSS) victim compensation can be accessed through:
Sec 357A CrPC / Sec 395 BNSS state victim compensation schemes are administered through SLSA/DLSA — they supplement Rule 12 and can provide compensation even before conviction or even if accused acquitted.
Q4. Form I (Rule 12 relief application) must be submitted with which four documents?
The four essential documents for Form I are: (1) Certified copy of FIR; (2) SC/ST Caste Certificate from Tehsildar/SDM; (3) Aadhaar card; (4) MLC/Medical Certificate (where applicable).
Q5. If the DM does not respond to Form I within 30 days, the paralegal should:
RTI to the district welfare office (PIO) is the immediate step — asking for status of Form I application. If no response even to RTI, escalate to First Appellate Authority and simultaneously approach DVMC.
Q6. DVMC engagement for systemic complaints requires filing written representation with:
Written representation to DM office 7 days before the quarterly DVMC meeting ensures it is placed before the committee for discussion — this creates an official record and triggers the committee's action duty.
Q7. SLSA can provide legal aid for which stage after Special Court verdict?
SLSA (State Legal Services Authority) provides legal aid for appeals/revision petitions in the High Court — ensuring victims can challenge inadequate sentences or acquittals.
Q8. RTI information about DVMC meetings — minutes and Action Taken Reports — is obtained from:
DVMC records (minutes, ATR, resolutions) are held at DM/Collector office — PIO at DM office or District SC/ST Welfare Department is the correct RTI target.
Q9. Free legal aid for SC/ST victims under DLSA is accessible:
Sec 12(c) LSA Act 1987 provides an absolute right to free legal services for SC/ST persons regardless of income — no means test is applicable.
Q10. For land dispossession atrocity cases, the primary relief under Rule 12 is:
Restoration of possession is the primary remedy for land dispossession under Rule 12 — the wrongfully dispossessed person gets their land back plus monetary compensation (minimum ₹25,000).
Q11. RTI to the state Social Welfare Department can be used to monitor:
RTI to Social Welfare Department for SC/ST Development Fund disbursements reveals whether compensation funds are being allocated and spent — useful for systemic advocacy and budget monitoring.
Q12. What should a PLV do immediately after receiving a DM acknowledgment of Form I?
The acknowledgment receipt is critical evidence — keep it, note the date, calculate the 30-day response deadline, and calendar a follow-up date to file RTI if no response within that window.
Q13. Lok Adalat is appropriate for which type of matter in atrocity-related cases?
Lok Adalat handles civil matters — land restoration and property recovery associated with atrocity cases can be pursued through Lok Adalat for faster resolution. Criminal atrocity charges are NOT compoundable at Lok Adalat.
Q14. Caste certificate for Rule 12 application must be obtained from:
SC/ST caste certificates are issued by the Tehsildar/SDM — a certified copy (original or attested) must accompany Form I to establish the victim's SC/ST identity.
Q15. After DVMC meeting, the paralegal should file RTI for:
Minutes and ATR create an accountability trail — if DVMC resolved to take action, the ATR proves whether action was taken. Non-compliance with ATR is grounds for further escalation.
Q16. Total Rule 12 compensation for gang rape is:
Gang rape attracts the highest Rule 12 compensation of ₹8,25,000 — same as death and permanent disability — reflecting the severity of the offence. Interim relief is 25% (₹2,06,250) on FIR registration.
Q17. DVMC representation from civil society/NGOs is enabled by:
Rule 17(3) provides for NGO/civil society representatives on DVMC — formal application to DM requesting inclusion as a member from the organisation enables direct participation.
Q18. Under the RTI Act 2005, the fee for a standard information request is:
Standard RTI application fee is ₹10, payable by Indian Postal Order, court fee stamp, or online. BPL cardholders are exempt from fees — SC/ST persons should check if their state provides exemption.
Q19. The primary purpose of obtaining acknowledgment receipt from DM on Form I submission is to:
The acknowledgment establishes the filing date — critical for calculating the 30-day response deadline (for interim relief) and for supporting any RTI or escalation complaint about non-response.
Q20. A PLV's complete toolkit for an atrocity case includes all of the following EXCEPT:
Settlement/negotiation between victim and accused is NOT in a paralegal's toolkit — atrocity offences are non-compoundable. The PLV's role is legal empowerment and access to justice, not compromise facilitation.
