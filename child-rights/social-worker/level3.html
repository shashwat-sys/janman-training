🔗 Child Protection Convergence Framework

The convergence framework establishes a multi-agency coordinated response to child protection — where Police, CWC, DCPU, Health, Education, Labour and DLSA work together under district administration oversight. No single department can effectively protect a child alone.

Core Principle
Child protection is a shared institutional responsibility. The framework ensures no child "falls through the cracks" between departments. Each department has defined roles, timelines, and convergence points with other departments.

🌟 Mission Vatsalya — Child Welfare Scheme

Mission Vatsalya (formerly Child Protection Services) is a centrally sponsored scheme under MoWCD for strengthening child protection systems across India.

Key Components:

  • Institutional Care: Funding for CCIs — Children's Homes, Shelter Homes, Observation Homes, Special Homes, Places of Safety
  • Non-Institutional Care: Foster care, sponsorship (financial support to families), adoption support
  • Service Delivery: DCPU staffing, CWC/JJB support, SCPCR/DCPCR functioning
  • Emergency Outreach: Open shelters, mobile teams for street children
  • Childline 1098: 24×7 emergency helpline — funded under Mission Vatsalya
Under Mission Vatsalya, a family below poverty line with a child at risk can receive sponsorship support (up to ₹4,000/month per child) to prevent institutionalisation. Social workers should actively identify and link eligible families to this scheme through DCPU.

📝 Documentation Standards: SIR and ICP

Social Investigation Report (SIR)

The SIR is the most critical document in a child's case file. It provides the CWC with information needed to determine the child's best interests.

SIR Contents (JJ Model Rules)
  • Child's personal details, age, education, health status
  • Family background — parents, siblings, economic condition
  • Circumstances leading to child's situation
  • Assessment of home safety (for potential restoration)
  • Risk factors — substance abuse in home, domestic violence, exploitation history
  • Child's own views and wishes
  • Recommendations for placement and rehabilitation
The SIR must be objective, based on home visits and interviews — NOT assumptions. The child's voice must be recorded. Timeline: ordinarily 15 days from CWC direction, but should be prepared as early as possible.

Individual Care Plan (ICP)

The ICP is a personalised plan for each child in institutional care, reviewed quarterly. It covers education, health, vocational training, family contact, restoration timeline, and long-term goals.

🔄 Complete Field Workflow — POCSO Case (OSC Worker's Guide)

  1. Receive referral / child arrives at OSC — Ensure immediate physical safety. Private space. Do NOT ask child to repeat their story multiple times.
  2. Medical assistance — Take child to hospital immediately. Ensure woman doctor for girl child. Do NOT make child bathe or change clothes. Request MLC.
  3. FIR support — Accompany family/child to police station or SJPU. Ensure child statement is recorded at residence/OSC, not at police station. Ensure no night detention.
  4. Produce before CWC within 24 hours — SJPU responsibility; OSC to coordinate and accompany. CWC to pass interim protection order.
  5. DCPU coordination — Request SIR preparation. Provide case details. Coordinate ICP for institutionalised child.
  6. DLSA coordination — Connect family to legal aid. Ensure victim compensation claim under NALSA/SVCS scheme.
  7. Counselling — Begin trauma-informed psychosocial support for child and family. Do NOT force disclosure. Allow child to set the pace.
  8. Court accompaniment — Support family/child during trial. Ensure in-camera proceedings. Coordinate with special public prosecutor.
  9. Follow-up — Monthly follow-up visits. Monitor school re-enrolment. Assess safety. Reconnect if support needed.
  10. MIS documentation — All case details entered in MIS within 24 hours. Maintain strict confidentiality. No identity disclosure.

📋 SOC Checklists for Field Practice

Police Checklist (POCSO case)

  • ☐ FIR registered immediately
  • ☐ Child not detained at police station at night
  • ☐ Medical examination arranged — woman doctor for girl
  • ☐ Child produced before CWC within 24 hours
  • ☐ Statement recorded at child's residence/place of choice
  • ☐ Child not confronted with accused
  • ☐ Identity not disclosed to media

CWC Checklist

  • ☐ Inquiry initiated upon production
  • ☐ Interim safety/placement order passed
  • ☐ SIR ordered from DCPU (15 days)
  • ☐ ICP reviewed quarterly
  • ☐ Restoration order only after home verification
  • ☐ Monthly CCI visits conducted

OSC Worker Checklist

  • ☐ Child's safety secured first
  • ☐ Medical examination facilitated
  • ☐ FIR support provided
  • ☐ CWC production coordinated
  • ☐ DLSA/legal aid connected
  • ☐ Counselling initiated
  • ☐ MIS entry done (confidentially)
  • ☐ Monthly follow-up scheduled
