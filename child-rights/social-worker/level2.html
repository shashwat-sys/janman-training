📜 Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015

The JJ Act, 2015 (amended in 2021) is the primary legislation governing both children in need of care and protection (CNCP) and children in conflict with the law (CCL). It replaced the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000.

👶 Child in Need of Care and Protection (CNCP)

Section 2(14) JJ Act — Definition of CNCP
A child is CNCP if the child: (a) is found without any home, settled place of abode, and without any ostensible means of subsistence; (b) is found begging, or is either a street child or a working child; (c) resides with a person (guardian/parent) who has threatened to kill or injure the child; (d) is mentally ill or is differently-abled or has illness with no support; (e) has parent/guardian who is unfit or incapacitated; (f) does not have parents or has missing parents; (g) is victim of, or is at risk of, abuse/neglect/exploitation; (h) is a victim of trafficking or is at risk of trafficking; (i) is a child marriage survivor or a child in forced labour.
Any child who is a victim of POCSO offence is automatically CNCP and must be produced before CWC within 24 hours. The CWC then determines suitable placement — restoration with family, fit person, foster care, or CCI.

🏛️ Child Welfare Committee (CWC)

Section 27 JJ Act — Composition of CWC
Each district shall have a Child Welfare Committee (CWC) consisting of:
  • 1 Chairperson (with social work background)
  • 4 members (at least 1 woman, 1 child expert)
Members hold quasi-judicial powers. Term: 3 years.

Key Powers and Functions of CWC (Sections 29-30)

The CWC is NOT a criminal court. It does not try the accused. Its sole purpose is the care and protection of the child. Never confuse CWC (child protection body) with JJB (juvenile justice body for CCL).

📋 District Child Protection Unit (DCPU)

Section 106 JJ Act — District Child Protection Unit
Every district shall have a DCPU to coordinate district-level child protection activities, maintain case records, prepare SIR and ICP, and facilitate restoration and rehabilitation.

DCPU Key Functions:

  • Social Investigation Report (SIR): Prepared within 15 days of a child being placed in institutional care. Contains family background, circumstances, risk assessment, recommendations for placement.
  • Individual Care Plan (ICP): Comprehensive plan for each child's rehabilitation — education, health, vocational training, family contact, restoration timeline.
  • Home Verification: Visits child's home before restoration to assess safety.
  • Follow-Up Visits: Monthly visits after restoration to monitor child's well-being.
  • Database maintenance: Records of all CNCP children — missing, trafficked, rescued, institutionalised.
  • Sponsorship coordination: Linking eligible children to Mission Vatsalya sponsorship scheme.

🏠 Child Care Institutions (CCIs)

CCIs under JJ Act include: Observation Homes, Special Homes, Places of Safety, Children's Homes, and Shelter Homes. After the 2021 amendment, ALL CCIs must be registered under the JJ Act — even those run by NGOs.

Observation Home
For children in conflict with law pending inquiry
Children's Home
For CNCP children requiring institutional care
Shelter Home
For immediate short-term shelter — children in street situations
Place of Safety
For children above 16 tried as adults, or for safety placements
Special Home
For children in conflict with law requiring long-term care
2021 Amendment
All CCIs must register under JJ Act; district magistrate has new oversight role

🔄 Restoration & Rehabilitation

Restoration (Section 40)

Restoration of a child to their family is the preferred outcome. It shall be done only after CWC satisfies itself that restoration is in the best interest of the child. DCPU prepares home verification report.

Restoration is NOT automatic. The home must be verified. The family/guardian must be willing and capable. There must be no risk of further harm. A post-restoration follow-up plan must be in place.

Rehabilitation Measures Available:

  • Family-Based Care: Restoration to parents/relatives; guardian appointment
  • Foster Care (Section 44): Child placed with approved foster family while efforts continue for restoration
  • Sponsorship (Section 45): Financial support to families to prevent institutionalisation (Mission Vatsalya)
  • Adoption (Chapter V): For children declared legally free for adoption by CWC; processed through CARA
  • After-Care (Section 46): Support for children leaving CCIs upon turning 18 — vocational training, residential support

👮 JJB — Juvenile Justice Board (for Children in Conflict with Law)

Section 4 JJ Act — Juvenile Justice Board
Each district shall have a JJB comprising a Metropolitan/Judicial Magistrate (1st Class) + 2 social workers (at least 1 woman). JJB handles all cases involving children alleged to have committed an offence.
A child in conflict with law (CCL) goes to JJB — NOT CWC. If a 10-year-old is accused of theft, they appear before JJB. If a 10-year-old is trafficked, they are CNCP and go to CWC. These are distinct bodies with distinct functions.

JJB Key Principles:

  • Bail-first approach (Section 12): Child to be released on bail unless there are reasonable grounds that release will bring them into association with known criminals
  • Child-friendly inquiry: No aggressive questioning; no handcuffs; no uniform for police/lawyers
  • Preliminary Assessment (Section 15): For heinous offences by child aged 16+, JJB conducts preliminary assessment on mental/physical capacity; may send to Children's Court if needed
