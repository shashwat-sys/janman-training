🏛️ POCSO Act 2012: Fundamentals

Mandatory reporting, child-friendly procedures, medical examination, and the OSC integrated response pathway

📋 Overview & Applicability

The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO) is a comprehensive law enacted to protect children below the age of 18 from sexual abuse and exploitation. It is gender-neutral — it protects all children regardless of sex — and provides for special child-friendly procedures throughout the legal process.

Section 2(d) — Definition of Child
A "child" means any person below the age of 18 years. The law is silent on the minimum age, meaning even infants are protected.
Enacted
2012; in force from 14 November 2012
Amended
POCSO Amendment Act 2019 — enhanced penalties including death penalty in aggravated penetrative sexual assault
Rules
POCSO Rules 2020 (replaced 2012 Rules) — child-friendly measures, victim support
Jurisdiction
Pan-India; special courts designated in each district

🔍 Types of Offences under POCSO

1. Penetrative Sexual Assault (Section 3)

Any person who does any of the following acts to a child is said to commit "penetrative sexual assault":

  • Penetration of penis into vagina, mouth, urethra or anus of child
  • Insertion of any object or body part (other than penis) into any of the above
  • Manipulation of child's body for penetration
  • Application of mouth to genital, anus or breast
Section 4 — Punishment for Penetrative Sexual Assault
Minimum 10 years (extendable to life imprisonment) + fine. After 2019 amendment: if victim is below 16 years — minimum 20 years extendable to life.

2. Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault (Section 5)

Penetrative sexual assault becomes "aggravated" when committed by police officer, armed forces, public servant, management/staff of jail/hospital/institution, relative, on child below 12 years, gang rape, pregnant child, disabled child, or child under mental/physical incapacity.

Section 6 — Punishment for Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault
Minimum 20 years (extendable to life/death) + fine payable to victim. 2019 amendment added death penalty as an option.

3. Sexual Assault (Section 7) — "Skin-to-Skin" Controversy Resolved

A person commits sexual assault if, with sexual intent, he touches vagina, penis, anus or breast of child; or makes the child touch these parts of any person; or does any other act with sexual intent involving physical contact without penetration.

The Bombay HC's 2021 controversial "skin-to-skin" judgment (Satish Ragde case) holding that touching through clothing is not sexual assault was overturned by the Supreme Court in Attorney General v. Satish (2021) 5 SCC 1. Any physical contact with sexual intent — with or without skin-to-skin contact — constitutes sexual assault.

4. Harassment (Section 11) — Sexual Harassment

Section 11 — Sexual Harassment
Includes: making sexually explicit remarks, showing pornographic material, making obscene gestures, stalking online or otherwise. Punishable under Section 12: up to 3 years + fine.

5. Use of Child for Pornography (Section 13)

Any person who uses a child for pornographic purposes shall be punishable with imprisonment up to 5 years (first conviction), 7 years (subsequent conviction) and fine. Storing child pornography under Section 15 after 2019 amendment is also an offence.

📞 Mandatory Reporting — Your Most Important Duty

Section 19 — Reporting of Offences
Any person (including a child) who apprehends that an offence under POCSO is likely to be committed or has knowledge that such an offence has been committed shall provide such information to the Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) or local police. This is a mandatory, not discretionary, obligation.
Section 21 — Failure to report by a person in charge of institution (school, hospital, CCI, etc.) is an offence punishable with up to 6 months imprisonment and/or fine. Failure to report by media, hotel, lodge, hospital — punishable with up to 1 year imprisonment.
As an OSC/social worker, if you come to know of a POCSO offence — through the child directly, through a family member, through community — you must report it immediately to SJPU or local police station. Never wait for the family's permission.

Procedure on Receiving Information (Section 19(6))

  • Police to record information in writing
  • Read over to informant
  • Signature to be taken
  • Copy to be given to informant
  • If child gives information, record in simple language child can understand

SJPU Obligations (Section 19(5-6))

  • Immediately provide care and protection to child
  • Take child to nearest hospital within 24 hours if needed
  • Produce child before CWC within 24 hours
  • Register FIR as applicable

🏥 Medical Examination of Child (Section 27)

Section 27 — Medical Examination
Medical examination of a child alleged to be victim of sexual offence shall be conducted as per Section 164A CrPC/BNSS. The examination shall be conducted by a woman doctor if the victim is a girl. No prior FIR or police complaint is required to seek medical examination — it can be done before FIR.
Key OSC Practice: Medical examination should NOT wait for FIR to be registered. Take the child directly to hospital. Ensure it is done by a woman doctor for girl children. MLC report must be prepared. Do NOT make the child bathe or change clothes before examination.
Who conducts
Registered medical practitioner (woman doctor for girl child)
When
Immediately / without delay; within 24 hours ideally
Consent
Child's consent (+ parent/guardian if available; not mandatory to wait)
Report
Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC) — to be given to police, copy to guardian

🎙️ Recording Statement of Child

Section 24 — Procedure for Recording Statement
The statement of a child shall be recorded at the child's residence or place of choice — NOT necessarily at the police station. A woman police officer shall record the statement. No child shall be detained at police station at night. No child to be brought in contact with accused.
Section 25 — Recording by Magistrate
The Magistrate records the child's statement under Section 164 CrPC/BNSS. The Magistrate shall use child-friendly language, shall not allow wearing of uniform, shall not intimidate the child.
Never confront the child with the accused. The child must never be asked to identify the accused in person. Video conferencing can be used for court testimony. The child's identity must never be disclosed (Section 23 POCSO + Section 228A IPC/BNS).

👩‍⚖️ Special Courts & Trial Procedure

State Governments designate Special Courts for each district for speedy trial of POCSO offences. The Special Public Prosecutor is appointed for each Special Court.

Time limit for trial
Ordinarily within 1 year of cognizance (Section 35)
Trial mode
In-camera trial (Section 37) — no public or press allowed
Identity
Identity of child never to be disclosed — Nipun Saxena guidelines
Presumption
Section 29: Rebuttable presumption of guilt against accused once penetrative sexual assault is established
Section 33 — Child-Friendly Procedures in Court
Court shall ensure child is not confronted by accused, questions put through judge, no aggressive cross-examination, breaks allowed, support person present, child-friendly atmosphere.

🏢 Role of the One Stop Centre (OSC)

The OSC (Sakhi) is a government-run centre co-located with public hospitals, providing integrated services to women and child victims of violence. Under POCSO, OSC has a critical convergence role:

ServiceOSC Function
Emergency ResponseReceive referrals from police, hospitals, community; ensure immediate safety
Medical AssistanceFacilitate medical examination; ensure woman doctor for girl child
Legal AidConnect victim to DLSA; assist in drafting complaint; explain rights
CounsellingPsychosocial first aid; trauma-informed counselling; family counselling
FIR SupportAccompany survivor to police station; ensure child-friendly procedures
CWC LiaisonEnsure child produced before CWC within 24 hours; coordinate with DCPU
ShelterShort-term shelter for those unable to return home safely
Follow-UpMonitor case progress; court accompaniment; long-term support
OSC Computer Operator's primary role: secure documentation / MIS entry. All case details to be entered confidentially. Identity of child NEVER to be shared publicly or on social media.

🔒 Confidentiality & Media Restrictions

Section 23 — Prohibition on Disclosure of Identity
No report in any media shall disclose the identity of a child — name, address, photograph, family details, school, neighbourhood or any other detail. Violation punishable with up to 6 months imprisonment. This applies to ALL media including social media.
Do NOT post photos, names, or case details of child victims on WhatsApp groups, Facebook, or any social media — even with good intentions. This is a criminal offence under Section 23 POCSO.
