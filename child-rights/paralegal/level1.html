Legal Framework: POCSO, JJ Act & Child Offences

Understanding the legal provisions that protect children in India

1. POCSO Act, 2012 — Child Sexual Abuse Protection

Key Sections for Paralegals

  • Section 3-6: Offences (penetrative abuse, non-penetrative abuse, sexual harassment)
  • Section 19: Mandatory reporting obligation — ANY person who suspects abuse MUST report to police
  • Section 24: Recording of child's statement — child-friendly, non-threatening environment required
  • Section 27: Medical examination — can be done without child's consent if abuse suspected
  • Section 39: Support person role — accompany child in proceedings
Offence Type Section Punishment Key Element
Penetrative Sexual Abuse Sec 3 7 years to life Any sexual act involving penetration
Aggravated Penetrative Abuse Sec 5 10 years to life By parent, custodian, repeat offense
Non-penetrative Sexual Abuse Sec 7 3-5 years Sexual act without penetration
Sexual Harassment Sec 11 3 years Unwanted sexual acts, comments, gestures
Section 19 POCSO mandates ANY person (including paralegals, teachers, healthcare workers) to report suspected abuse to police immediately. Non-reporting can attract prosecution.

2. Juvenile Justice Act, 2015 — Child Welfare & Justice

Child in Need of Care & Protection (CNCP) — Sec 2(14)

A child who lacks proper guardianship, is abused, neglected, abandoned, trafficked, or living on streets. Handled by Child Welfare Committee.

Child Welfare Committee (CWC) — Sec 27-30

District-level body. Hears cases of CNCP children. Paralegals assist by representing children or filing representations.

District Child Protection Unit (DCPU) — Sec 106

Implements child welfare schemes. Registers CNCP cases. Paralegals can advocate for welfare schemes here.

Juvenile Justice Board (JJB) — Sec 4-8

Handles children in conflict with law (accused of crime). Focuses on rehabilitation, not punishment.

As a paralegal, you can accompany a child victim to CWC proceedings, explain the process in simple language, and file written representations on behalf of the child's welfare.

3. Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 — Child Offences

BNS Section Offence Punishment Related IPC (old)
Sec 74 Assault/use of force on woman/child 3 months to 2 years IPC 351-352
Sec 75 Sexual harassment 3 years IPC 354
Sec 94 Trafficking of person 7 years to life IPC 370
Sec 95 Trafficking of child (aggravated) 10 years to life IPC 371

4. Prohibition of Child Marriage Act (PCMA), 2006

Paralegal Action Points

Section 12: Child marriage is void (has no legal validity). Parties have right to void it at any time even after attaining majority (up to 2 years after attaining 18).

Section 13: Grounds for annulment of child marriage — lack of consent, fraud, coercion. Petition can be filed by child, parent, guardian, or any interested person.

Prevention: Paralegal can be part of Bal Vivah Pratirodh Dal (Anti-Child Marriage Task Force) to identify and prevent child marriages in community.

5. Child Labour (Prohibition & Regulation) Amendment Act, 2016

Complete Ban Below 14

NO work permitted for children below 14 years in any occupation. This includes domestic work.

Hazardous Work Ban Below 18

Children 14-18 cannot work in hazardous industries: mining, manufacturing, construction.

Enforcement

Report violations to Labour Department. Child can be rescued and referred to CWC for care.

6. Right to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act, 2009

Right Details
Age Group 6-14 years are entitled to free and compulsory education
Non-admission/Exclusion Prohibited School cannot refuse admission to any child; cannot expel without due process
Neighbourhood School Child has right to attend school in neighborhood area; state must provide within 1 km
No Fee/Cost Free education; school cannot demand fees from poor families
Paralegals can help families file RTI to get school records of excluded children, and approach CWC if child is out of school due to neglect or abuse.
