Victim Support, Legal Aid & Compensation

Securing justice and support for child victims

1. Section 164 CrPC/BNSS — Recording of Victim Statement

Who Records the Statement?

District Magistrate (or authorized officer) records the statement under oath. Can be recorded at police station, hospital, or child's home — wherever the child is comfortable.

Child-Friendly Procedures

  • Non-threatening environment
  • Presence of trusted adult
  • No aggressive questioning
  • Recorded in language child understands

Evidentiary Value

  • Statement is direct evidence
  • Can be used in court proceedings
  • Critical in sexual abuse cases
  • Reduces need for repeat statements

2. Legal Aid Entitlements

Provision Details Paralegal Role
Sec 12 LSA Act 1987 Legal aid entitlement for poor & marginalized Help file application with DLSA
Child Victims Auto-Entitled ALL child abuse victims automatically entitled to legal aid No means test required; inform victim of rights
DLSA Obligation District Legal Services Authority MUST provide lawyer File complaint if DLSA denies aid
Child victims don't need to prove poverty — legal aid is automatic. Visit DLSA (usually at District Court) with FIR copy and get lawyer assigned for FREE.

3. NALSA Victim Compensation Scheme

Who Can Apply?

  • Direct victim of crime (child)
  • Parent/guardian on behalf of child
  • NGO/paralegal on behalf of child (with child's consent or court order)
Timeline Action
After conviction Court can award compensation under CrPC Sec 357 (now BNSS 395)
1 month of crime Application to Victim Compensation Board for INTERIM compensation
Any time during trial Application for FINAL compensation (after conviction)
Child victims get ENHANCED compensation amounts (2-3x higher) compared to adults for same crime. Interim compensation available within 1 month of filing application.

4. State Victim Compensation Scheme (SVCS)

Section 357A CrPC/BNSS 395 — State Compensation

Each state has its own victim compensation scheme. The state compensates victim even if:

  • Offender is not convicted
  • Offender cannot be identified
  • Fine cannot be recovered

Crime Type Compensation Amount (Approx) Enhanced for Child
Sexual Abuse ₹50,000-200,000 ₹150,000-500,000
Severe Injury ₹25,000-100,000 ₹75,000-250,000
Trafficking ₹100,000-500,000 ₹300,000-1,000,000

5. POCSO Rules 2020 — Compensation Provisions

Interim Compensation

Granted immediately after FIR registration. Amounts typically ₹5,000-25,000. Meets immediate expenses.

Final Compensation

After conviction. Amounts ₹50,000-500,000+ depending on harm. Awarded by court.

Medical Expense Reimbursement

Full reimbursement of medical exam, treatment, counseling costs without limits.

6. Section 357B CrPC/BNSS 395 — Compensation in Rape/CSA Cases

In rape or child sexual assault convictions, court must award compensation IN ADDITION to fine. Compensation is separate & guaranteed.

Compensation is NOT charity — it's the state's legal obligation to the victim. Paralegals must actively help victims claim compensation.

7. Support Person Role — Section 39 POCSO

Duty Example Do NOT Do
Accompany child Be present during police questioning, court hearing Leave child alone during proceedings
Explain proceedings "The judge will ask questions to understand what happened" Coach child on answers; suggest what to say
Facilitate communication Help child communicate fear, pain, needs Speak on behalf of child in testimony
Provide comfort Hold hand, reassure before difficult questioning Give evidence about what happened
As a support person, you CANNOT give evidence about the abuse. Your role is purely to support the child emotionally and logistically. The child must testify themselves.

8. CWC Proceedings — Paralegal Assistance

How Paralegals Help CNCP Children Before CWC

  • Document the child's situation (neglect, abuse, exploitation)
  • File petition/representation with CWC on behalf of child
  • Represent child's best interests at CWC hearing
  • Advocate for specific relief: shelter, school, compensation, rehabilitation
  • Monitor implementation of CWC orders
  • Appeal if CWC decision is unfavorable
