Field Practice: Complaints, RTI & Community Action

Practical skills for on-ground paralegal work

1. Drafting Complaint to Police

Essential Elements of a Police Complaint

  • Date & Time: When the crime occurred
  • Location: Exact place where abuse/crime happened
  • Victim Details: Name, age, address, contact info
  • Accused Details: Name, relation to victim, address if known
  • Narrative: Chronological account of what happened (clear, factual, no opinions)
  • Witnesses: Names of anyone who saw or knows about the crime
  • Physical Evidence: Any injuries, torn clothing, medical reports
  • Relevant Law: Which sections of POCSO/BNS apply
Always file written complaint. Get a copy of FIR after filing. Keep FIR number for all future communications with police.
Verification of FIR: Visit police station 2-3 days after filing to confirm FIR is registered. If police refuse to register FIR, escalate to Senior Police Officer or approach NHRC.

2. Drafting Representation to CWC

Identify Child's Needs: Is child in need of shelter, schooling, medical care, rehabilitation?
Gather Evidence: Collect documents — medical reports, school records, police FIR, photographs of injury/home.
Draft Representation: Format: Letterhead → CWC Chair → Petition → Child's details → Statement of facts → Legal basis (JJ Act, POCSO) → Relief sought.
Relief to Seek: Shelter (Sec 40), education (Sec 41), rehabilitation (Sec 42), compensation, reunification with safe family member.
File & Follow Up: File original + 2 copies. Attend CWC hearing. Monitor compliance of CWC order.

3. RTI Applications for Child Welfare Data

Information Sought Authority to Approach Purpose
Status of registered CNCP case CWC/DCPU Track child's progress in system
FIR status, investigation progress Police Station Ensure investigation is proceeding
Case status in criminal court District Court Track case progress, schedule next hearing
School records, attendance School Principal Evidence if child excluded from school
Child protection committee decisions Village/Ward Admin Track community-level interventions
RTI exemptions for child identity: Authorities CANNOT disclose child's name/photo/location publicly. RTI should omit child's identity from response OR ask for response in sealed cover.

4. Approaching NHRC/SHRC for Child Rights Violations

Who Can File?

Child (through guardian), parent, NGO, paralegal, any concerned person

What to Include?

Detailed memo, supporting documents (FIR, medical reports, school records, photographs), legal basis, relief sought

NHRC/SHRC Format

Written petition to Chairman, with certified copies of all documents. Free filing. No legal representation required.

5. Childline 1098 Protocol

When to Call Childline

  • Child is in immediate danger (abuse, trafficking, sexual assault)
  • Child is lost/separated from family
  • Child is in conflict with law (needs legal aid)
  • Child needs emergency shelter/food/medical care
  • Child needs counseling/emotional support
Step Action Paralegal Role
1. Call 1098 24-hour hotline — speak to trained counselor Call on behalf of child if child cannot communicate
2. Describe Situation Counselor notes details of child's situation Provide accurate info; mention any injury/urgency
3. Immediate Action Childline dispatches team to rescue/help child Remain available; provide location details
4. Follow-up Childline refers child to CWC/shelter/medical care Track referral; monitor child's welfare

6. Community-Level Child Protection

Village Child Protection Committee (VCPC)

Village-level committee. Identifies at-risk children. Paralegals can serve as member or advisor.

Spotting At-Risk Children

Signs: unexplained injuries, poor nutrition, excessive absenteeism, behavioral changes, child labor, early marriage.

Mandatory Reporter Obligation

POCSO Sec 19: You MUST report suspected abuse to police. Non-reporting can lead to prosecution.

7. Prevention of Child Marriage

Paralegal Role as Bal Vivah Pratirodh Dal Member

  • Monitor communities for child marriage attempts
  • Approach families considering child marriage; explain harm & legal consequences
  • File police complaint if child marriage is being planned/performed
  • Apply for injunction (Sec 13 PCMA) to prevent marriage
  • Help child file petition to void existing child marriage

8. Emergency Legal Aid from DLSA

Visit DLSA Office: Usually at District Court. No appointment needed for emergency cases.
Bring Documents: FIR (if available), child's ID proof, guardian's ID, medical reports if any.
Explain Situation: Tell DLSA officer about child's legal need (criminal case, compensation, CWC representation).
Lawyer Assignment: DLSA assigns lawyer immediately for emergency cases (child victims are automatic).
Timeline: Lawyer should meet child within 24-48 hours for urgent cases.
